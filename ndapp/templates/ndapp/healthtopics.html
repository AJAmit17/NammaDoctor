Dermatology
  • Although primarily concerned with skin health, dermatological conditions can sometimes reflect underlying heart diseases.
  • Skin cancer
  • Dermatitis
  • Allergies and sensitivities
Endocrinology
  • Endocrine disorders like diabetes and thyroid dysfunction can impact cardiovascular health.
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Adrenal disorders
Gastroenterology
  • Gastrointestinal conditions like inflammatory bowel disease can have indirect effects on heart health due to chronic inflammation.
  • Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)
  • Arrhythmias
  • Liver diseases (hepatitis, cirrhosis)
Neurology
  • Neurological conditions can influence heart health through the autonomic nervous system, affecting heart rate and blood pressure regulation.
  • Stroke
  • Epilepsy
  • Migraines and headaches
Orthopedics
  • Orthopedic surgeries or conditions can impact the cardiovascular system during and after procedures due to stress and potential immobility.
  • Arthritis
  • Spinal disorders
  • Sports injuries
Pulmonology
  • Pulmonary diseases like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) can strain the heart due to decreased lung function, leading to pulmonary hypertension.
  • Pneumonia
  • Lung cancer
Obstetrics and Gynecology
  • Pregnant women with pre-existing heart conditions require specialized care to manage risks during pregnancy.
  • Menstrual disorders
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
  • Endometriosis
Pediatrics
  • Congenital heart defects are common in pediatrics, requiring specialized care and surgeries to address structural issues.
  • Childhood vaccinations
  • Pediatric diseases (measles, mumps, chickenpox)
  • Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)
Oncology
  • Certain cancer treatments, like chemotherapy, can have cardiotoxic effects and increase the risk of heart diseases.
  • Cancer treatment options (chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy)
  • Palliative care and pain management
Infectious Diseases
  • Some infections, such as certain viral infections, can cause inflammation of the heart (myocarditis) and potentially lead to heart diseases.
  • Bacterial infections (tuberculosis, strep throat)
  • Parasitic infections (malaria, Lyme disease)
Mental Health
  • Psychological stress and mental health disorders can contribute to hypertension and increase the risk of heart diseases.
  • Depression
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Schizophrenia
Nephrology
  • Kidney health plays a crucial role in maintaining blood pressure and fluid balance, impacting cardiovascular health.
  • Kidney stones
  • Arrhythmias
  • Dialysis and kidney transplantation
Rheumatology
  • Autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis can have systemic effects, including inflammation that may affect the heart and blood vessels.
  • Autoimmune diseases (rheumatoid arthritis, lupus)
  • Joint and connective tissue disorders
